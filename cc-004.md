## Rationale

The 2021 guideline was authored when the first HPV-vaccinated cohorts were below screening age in most countries, and vaccination-modulated screening was not yet a programmatic question. The guideline therefore does not mention vaccination status as an input to screening decisions. The omission is temporal, not deliberate. By 2026, cohorts vaccinated under WHO's 2014 scale-up recommendation are approaching screening age in some jurisdictions, and the question of whether and how vaccination status should modulate screening eligibility, interval, or triage is becoming operationally live.

**The selected resolution is `no-input-inherit-assumption`: continue the textualist's non-handling of vaccination status.** Three reasons.

**First, regime consistency.** cc-000 declares the epidemiological regime this case study assumes: "HPV vaccination coverage in the aging cohort: low to moderate. The jurisdiction has introduced HPV vaccination but the first cohorts have not yet aged into screening eligibility in large numbers." In the declared regime, nearly every screening-age woman is vaccination-status "unvaccinated or unknown." A DAK input that captures the status would be overwhelmingly populated with the same value and would not modulate decisions. The input adds complexity without producing distinct behaviour.

**Second, no guidance to encode.** The 2021-07 guideline, the 2021-12 mRNA addendum, and the 2024-06 dual-stain addendum are all silent on vaccination-modulated screening. The 2022 SAGE position paper on single-dose HPV vaccination is a vaccination recommendation, not a screening recommendation — it does not modify any screening cascade element. The evidence on how vaccinated cohorts should be screened differently is accruing (HPV FOCAL long-term follow-up, Costa Rica vaccine trial), but it has not yet produced guideline-level recommendations. A purposive DAK that encoded vaccination-modulated rules now would be inventing screening policy ahead of WHO. That is the `add-input-with-modulated-rules` option, rejected.

**Third, the stub-input option is not obviously better.** `add-input-inert-stub` adds a `VaccinationStatus` field that the DMN rules ignore via don't-care cells (`-`). This was briefly considered as a forward-compatible structural change — the cascade would be ready to accept vaccination-modulated rules when the guideline catches up. It was rejected for two reasons: (a) the future guideline's data model is unknown (single-dose vs multi-dose, vaccine type matters or not, time since vaccination matters or not), and committing to a particular input schema now risks picking the wrong one; (b) adding an inert input creates maintenance and testing burden (the field has to be documented in the CDD, carried through the DMN, populated by L3 implementations, verified in tests) for no current functional benefit. Deferring until the guideline specifies both the input schema and the modulated rules is the lower-regret choice.

**Why this is an `inference` entry rather than a `scope` concern.** cc-000 already declares "HPV vaccination decisions are out of scope" at the scope level. This entry is narrower: it is about whether vaccination **status as a modifier of screening decisions** should be added as a DAK input. That is an interpretive inference about a silence in the guideline — WHO has not yet spoken on vaccination-modulated screening, and this entry documents the L2's decision to mirror that silence rather than anticipate it.

**Why `evidence_volatile: true`.** The evidence is moving. HPV FOCAL long-term follow-up, Costa Rica vaccine trial, the 2022 SAGE single-dose position paper, and emerging cohort-level surveillance all point toward eventually-different screening strategies for vaccinated cohorts. When that evidence crystallises into WHO guidance, this entry's `selected` should flip (most likely to `add-input-with-modulated-rules` with specifics aligned to the new guideline). The volatile flag makes this entry quickly findable when the trigger event occurs.

## Implementation Notes

* **No purposive DMN change on account of this entry.** The textualist's lack of a `VaccinationStatus` input transfers to the purposive DMN unchanged.
* **When the regime changes, cc-000 is revised first, then cc-004.** If a future version of this case study targets a jurisdiction with high vaccination coverage in the screening-age cohort, cc-000's epidemiological regime declaration should change first. cc-004 should then be re-examined — in a high-coverage regime, the rationale for `no-input-inherit-assumption` no longer holds, and one of the other options becomes preferable.
* **The "aggressive posture" implication.** In high-vaccination-coverage settings, shorter screening intervals produce diminishing true-positive yield at the cost of increasing false positives and unnecessary procedures. The "aggressive" policy posture that cc-005 resolves on (upper bound of the interval range) happens to also be the posture that makes sense in a future high-vaccination regime — shorter intervals would be **more** aggressive in the current regime but **less** evidence-aligned in the future regime. This is a coincidence of the current resolution rather than an intentional hedge; it is worth noting because a reader encountering both cc-004 and cc-005 may wonder whether the upper-bound interval resolution in cc-005 was chosen with vaccination modulation in mind. It was not.
* **Future-revision trigger.** If WHO issues vaccination-modulated screening guidance — most likely in the next Phase 3 or Phase 4 update of the 2021 guideline — this entry becomes `superseded`. The replacement entry should cite the specific revision and encode the vaccination input schema the revision specifies.
* **Interaction with cc-007 (sexual exposure precondition).** Vaccinated cohorts in the age range where cc-007's "over-screening of never-sexually-active individuals" applies may have an even larger over-screening margin (lower HPV exposure, lower per-screen yield). This is a second-order effect and does not modify cc-007's resolution, but a reader working through both entries should note the interaction.
