## Selected

The textualist and the first-case-study purposive DMN both use `ClientIsWoman`, populated from a patient-level `Sex = Female` determination, as the population element in the Eligible For Screening decision. This mirrors the 2021 guideline's Recommendation 1 language ("women") verbatim.

The primary reason is evidence-base fidelity. Rec 1 is the output of a GRADE process whose trial base — HPV primary screening trials — enrolled predominantly cisgender women. Committing the purposive L2 to a broader population encoding at the decision-logic level would assert a claim at higher evidential stringency than the trial base currently supports. The textualist-mirroring choice keeps the L2's stringency aligned with the recommendation's own.

`Sex` is a routinely captured data element in public-sector EHRs and paper registers across the target deployment landscape, which keeps the purposive L2 implementable in the jurisdictional regime declared in [cc-000](cc-000.html).

## Deferred: `HasCervix` as a purposive refinement

`HasCervix` is retained as a candidate purposive refinement for a future authoring pass or a future WHO guideline revision. It would encode the Section 3.1 broader population ("transgender men and non-binary and intersex individuals who have a cervix") as a data element the DAK consumes directly, rather than routing that population through a separate outreach workflow.

For the canonical treatment of the deferred element — the expected shape of the candidate CDD row, the three reasons it is not a first-pass row, the L3 work that would be required if it were pursued, and the two conditions under which it would become a row — see [`dictionary.md` § "Deferred as purposive refinement: `HasCervix`"](dictionary.html#deferred-as-purposive-refinement-hascervix).

## Conditions under which this entry would be revisited

1. A future purposive DMN authoring pass chooses to rename `ClientIsWoman` → `HasCervix` as a semantic refinement. This entry's `selected` would then become `sex-female (textualist); has-cervix (purposive)` and the CDD would gain a row for `HasCervix` alongside the existing `Sex` row.
2. A future WHO guideline revision broadens Rec 1's population language on the basis of accumulating trial evidence that includes non-cisgender-women populations, at which point the L2 harmonises with the revised recommendation and this entry is superseded.

Neither condition is met as of the first-case-study resolution.
