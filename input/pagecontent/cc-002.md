## Rationale

The 2021 guideline and the 2024-06 addendum both use "women living with HIV" as an undifferentiated boolean. No recommendation in 2021-07 stratifies by CD4 count, viral suppression duration, or ART adherence. The 2024-06 publication explicitly deferred further WLHIV recommendations on grounds of insufficient evidence. A purposive L2 that encoded finer stratification would be encoding a recommendation WHO has not made — inventing policy rather than interpreting it. The textualist's current encoding (`LivingWithHIV: boolean`) is both faithful to the guideline and operationally actionable. No DMN change is required for the purposive.

**Why `inference` and not `disambiguation`.** The guideline is not ambiguous about WLHIV — it is clear, using the boolean concept throughout. The interpretive act here is the inference to *not* add finer stratification despite emerging evidence that would support it. That is a choice made over a silence: the guideline is silent on CD4/VL/ART because WHO has not yet issued guidance there, and this entry documents the L2's decision to mirror that silence rather than anticipate future guidance.

**Why the patient-level boolean is the right granularity *now*.** In differentiated service delivery contexts, "living with HIV" is a spectrum (undiagnosed, newly diagnosed, on ART but not virally suppressed, virally suppressed <6 months, durably suppressed). The cervical cancer risk profile differs materially across these states, and ESTAMPA and African cohort studies are generating evidence that durably-suppressed WLHIV may eventually merit general-population screening intervals. But "eventually" is not "now." The current evidence base supports the direction of travel but not specific thresholds, and the 2024-06 publication's explicit deferral is WHO saying the same thing. Encoding a threshold ahead of guidance creates a policy artifact that is neither faithful to current WHO recommendations nor generalisable across deployment contexts.

**Cascade-wide uniformity.** The textualist applies the WLHIV modifier uniformly across Eligibility, Due For Screening, Triage, and Follow-up decisions. A purposive encoding could in principle use different HIV-related inputs at different points (e.g., viral suppression status at Treatment Decision). The decision not to decompose is consistent with the guideline, which itself applies the boolean uniformly. If a future WHO revision introduces decision-point-specific HIV stratification, the decomposition would be driven by that revision rather than pre-emptively.

**Rejected alternatives.** `stratified-cd4-vl-art` anticipates guidance WHO has declined to issue; not adoptable now. `boolean-plus-viral-suppression` is a forward-compatible stub that was briefly considered but ultimately rejected: adding an unused input to the purposive DMN adds maintenance and testing burden without functional improvement, and the eventual stratified guideline may not use viral-suppression-binary as its threshold anyway (it may use duration-of-suppression, CD4 trajectory, or something else). Leaving the cascade structure clean and deferring the input addition until the guideline specifies it is the lower-regret choice.

**Why `evidence_volatile: true`.** ESTAMPA and ongoing WLHIV cohort studies are actively generating evidence that may support finer stratification in a future guideline revision. When that revision occurs, this entry's `selected` should flip and the DMN will need new inputs and rules. The flag lets a future reviewer find the entry quickly when the trigger event occurs.

## Implementation Notes

- **No purposive DMN change on account of this entry.** The textualist encoding transfers to the purposive DMN unchanged. The `LivingWithHIV` input, the WLHIV-specific eligibility rules (`DecisionRule_1925hkc`, `DecisionRule_1gyepae`), the null catch-all (`DecisionRule_catchall_s_nullhiv`), and the triage and follow-up rules that consume WLHIV remain as encoded.
- **Audit the null-HIV catch-all during purposive authoring.** The existing `DecisionRule_catchall_s_nullhiv` should be confirmed to return a clinically safe default — likely "do not proceed to screening until HIV status is known" rather than a silent fall-through to the general-population path. This is a fidelity check, not a rule change.
- **L3 integration with HIV care systems.** In the epidemiological regime declared in `cc-000` (moderate-to-high HIV prevalence), a substantial fraction of screening-age women will be WLHIV, and their HIV status is typically documented in a separate HIV care system (SmartCare in Zambia, HIV-module-in-OpenMRS elsewhere). L3 implementers will need to consume HIV status from that system via FHIR or another integration mechanism. This is an L3 concern, not a register concern, but it is flagged here because it is non-trivial and the L3 implementability may influence when the DAK can be deployed at scale.
- **Future-revision trigger.** If WHO issues WLHIV stratification guidance at CD4 count, viral suppression duration, or ART adherence, this entry becomes `superseded` and a new entry (likely `cc-NNN-wlhiv-stratification`) should be authored with the new inputs and rules. The `evidence_volatile` flag is the signal that this trigger is expected rather than speculative.
- **The "double follow-up" textualist fidelity gap** (Rec 33 specifies two 12-month post-treatment retests for WLHIV; the textualist collapses to one) is tracked separately in [`index.md`](index.md) "Deferred and out of scope" and is not modified by this entry. A future textualist correction paired with a new register entry (likely categorised as `correction`) will address it.
