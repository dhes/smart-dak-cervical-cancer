Selected

The textualist and the first-case-study purposive DMN both use ClientIsWoman, populated from a patient-level Sex = Female determination, as the population element in the Eligible For Screening decision. This mirrors the 2021 guideline's Recommendation 1 language ("women") verbatim.

The primary reason is evidence-base fidelity. Rec 1 is the output of a GRADE process whose trial base — HPV primary screening trials — enrolled predominantly cisgender women. Committing the purposive L2 to a broader population encoding at the decision-logic level would assert a claim at higher evidential stringency than the trial base currently supports. The textualist-mirroring choice keeps the L2's stringency aligned with the recommendation's own.

Sex is a routinely captured data element in public-sector EHRs and paper registers across the target deployment landscape, which keeps the purposive L2 implementable in the jurisdictional regime declared in cc-000.

Deferred: HasCervix as the convergent refinement

The Sex = Female / ClientIsWoman encoding leaves two naive-reader instincts unanswered at the decision-logic level. Both resolve to the same candidate element and are deferred together.

Broader — admit transgender men, non-binary, and intersex individuals with a cervix. Section 3.1 of the 2021-07 guideline ("Programme considerations", p. 21) treats this population in body narrative — not a numbered GRADE recommendation, not a footnote. That language sits at lower evidential stringency than Rec 1, whose trial base (HPV primary screening trials) enrolled predominantly cisgender women. Broadening the L2 at the decision-logic level would assert a claim at higher stringency than the trial base currently supports.

Narrower — exclude women who have had a total hysterectomy. The 2021-07 guideline is silent on hysterectomy as a screening-eligibility criterion; "hysterectom-" appears only in Annex 5 as a surgical specimen type and as a rare treatment complication. The L2 mirrors what the guideline asserts, not what it assumes to be too clinically obvious to say. The "don't screen a patient without a cervix" filter lives at the encounter as clinical common sense, not as a DAK decision rule.

Why the same element serves both. A boolean HasCervix, populated from an anatomical-inventory signal rather than from Sex, simultaneously admits a trans man with a cervix and excludes a post-hysterectomy woman. One element, two filter effects, one Kind-3 candidate.

Why it is deferred in either direction. Deployment reality is the binding shared ground: no current LMIC public-sector EHR (Bahmni, eTracker, SmartCare, Impilo, iHRIS, OpenMRS-based systems) carries a BodyStructure or anatomical-inventory field, and hysterectomy history is not reliably surfaced at registration in the way Sex is. The per-direction grounds above are additional reasons; the deployment-reality ground is what makes HasCervix non-implementable today in either role.

For the canonical treatment of the deferred element — the expected shape of the candidate CDD row, the L3 work that would be required if it were pursued, and the conditions under which it would become a row — see dictionary.md § "Deferred as purposive refinement: HasCervix".

Conditions under which this entry would be revisited

  1. A future purposive DMN authoring pass chooses to substitute ClientIsWoman → HasCervix as a semantic refinement. This entry's selected would then become sex-female (textualist); has-cervix (purposive) and the CDD would gain a row for HasCervix alongside the existing Sex row.
  2. A future WHO guideline revision addresses population scope at Recommendation-level stringency — either broadening Rec 1 on the basis of accumulating trial evidence that includes non-cisgender-women populations, or narrowing it by naming hysterectomy status explicitly as an eligibility criterion — at which point the L2 harmonises with the revised recommendation and this entry is superseded.
  3. The deployment-landscape EHR profile shifts to carry anatomical-inventory or hysterectomy-history data as routinely as Sex, removing the binding implementability constraint documented in dictionary.md § "Deferred as purposive refinement: HasCervix".

None of these conditions is met as of the first-case-study resolution.
