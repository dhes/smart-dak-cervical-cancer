cc-008: HIV Status Absent/Unknown

cc-008 — HIV status absent/unknown: semantics and handling

Field Value
Entry ID cc-008
Category inference
Title HIV status absent/unknown: semantics and handling
Status proposed
Date 2026-04-20
Authors dan-heslinga
Selected fail-closed-purposive-reveal-gap-textualist
L1 source WHO 2021 Cervical Cancer Screening Guideline (2nd ed.)
Related entries cc-000, cc-002, cc-005

Rationale

The data element Living With HIV is typed as a boolean in the CDD, with true / false / null as the declared input options. The 2021-07 guideline and the 2024-06 addendum use "women living with HIV" as an undifferentiated boolean (see cc-002) but are silent on how to handle women for whom HIV status is absent or unknown at the point of care. That silence is not academic: in real deployments the boolean conflates at least three distinct real-world states.

  • Documented negative. The client has been tested for HIV and the result is negative in the consumed record.
  • Never tested. The client has no HIV test of record and no prior disclosure; status is genuinely unknown.
  • Records incomplete. The client may have a positive or negative result documented elsewhere, but the data available to the screening decision point does not include it.

Only the first of these is unambiguously "not WLHIV." The second and third are absent information, not negative results. Treating all three as false under-screens any woman who is in fact WLHIV but undocumented at the screening encounter — a material risk in the moderate-to-high HIV prevalence epidemiological regime declared in cc-000. Treating all three as true over-screens the general population and consumes WLHIV-pathway resources that may be scarce. Neither default is neutral.

The guideline's silence on this question is expected rather than surprising. WHO/IARC set clinical recommendations at population level; the operational question of what counts as WLHIV at point of care when the EHR doesn't say is exactly the kind of decision a DAK and its register are built to resolve. This entry resolves it.

Why inference and not disambiguation or specification. The guideline is not ambiguous about WLHIV — it is clear what the WLHIV pathway is. It is also not under-specified in the specification sense: there is no evidence-bounded range of clinical values from which the DAK must pick a point. The interpretive act here is an inference over a silence: the guideline does not address absent HIV status at all, and the DAK must decide what to do. This matches the shape of cc-006 (pregnancy silence) and cc-007 (sexual exposure silence).

The selected resolution is fail-closed-purposive-reveal-gap-textualist. This is a two-part decision tied to the methodology's textualist/purposive distinction.

Textualist: reveal the gap. A strict textualist encodes only what the guideline says. The guideline does not address absent HIV status, so the textualist DMN should not invent a rule for it. The existing DecisionRule_catchall_s_nullhiv in the textualist is an authorship artifact that silently fills a guideline silence — it paints over the gap the textualist exists to expose. Removing that rule in the textualist restores fidelity: calls with LivingWithHIV = null will either match no rule (returning null outputs) or, where the age-based over-49 rule also matches, will fail the UNIQUE hit policy at the runtime. Either manifestation is informative. The textualist's job is to expose the gap, not to fill it.

Purposive: fail-closed. The purposive DMN makes the policy call. The selected policy is fail-closed: when LivingWithHIV is null, return EligibleForScreening = false and ScreeningInterval = null, signalling to the operator that eligibility cannot be determined without HIV status. This is implemented by keeping a narrowed version of DecisionRule_catchall_s_nullhiv (narrowed so it does not overlap the over-49 rule) rather than by routing null HIV through the general-population or WLHIV pathway. The rationale is threefold.

  • Most conservative clinically. In moderate-to-high prevalence settings, undocumented WLHIV are a material fraction of the screening-age population. Routing null HIV to the general-population pathway (start age 30, 10-year interval) under-screens them. Fail-closed refuses to make a recommendation without the necessary input, which is the safer error mode in this regime.
  • Flags missingness to upstream systems. A fail-closed response is a signal, not a silent default. It tells the L3 implementer or the point-of-care workflow "HIV status is required here and was not provided," which is the correct behaviour when the DAK cannot reach a safe recommendation.
  • Reversible at country adaptation. A country with high HIV status documentation coverage (well-integrated HIV care records, reliable disclosure workflows) may reasonably flip this to null-as-false or null-as-true. The policy lives in this register entry and in a single purposive DMN rule; the country adaptation is a bounded change rather than a cascade rewrite.

Rejected alternatives.

  • null-routes-to-general-pop (translate null to false in the DMN rule structure, typically by loosening the HIV input in rules 1/2/3 from false to not(true)). Clinically weaker: under-screens undocumented WLHIV. Operationally forgiving: everyone gets some screening recommendation. Appropriate in settings with low HIV prevalence or reliable HIV status documentation; inappropriate in the epidemiological regime declared in cc-000.
  • null-routes-to-wlhiv (translate null to true, or mirror the WLHIV pathway at nulls by default). Most conservative at the individual level in high-prevalence settings: no WLHIV is missed. Costly at the population level: over-screens the general-population share of the null-HIV cohort and consumes scarce HPV test capacity and follow-up slots. May be defensible as a country-adaptation choice in very-high-prevalence settings; not the DAK-level default.
  • crash-and-document (leave the current overlap between the over-49 rule and the null-HIV catch-all in place and document the Camunda UNIQUE-policy failure as the system's answer to null HIV). Rejected as a purposive choice: a runtime failure is not a policy, it is a bug. Fail-closed achieves the same intent ("refuse to recommend without HIV status") with a graceful deterministic response rather than a stack trace.

Implementation Notes

  • Textualist DMN change (applied). DecisionRule_catchall_s_nullhiv has been removed from textualist.dmn#CC.A.eligibility_t. This restores the textualist's fidelity to the guideline's silence on absent HIV status and, as a side effect, resolves the previous Camunda UNIQUE-policy runtime failure on {ClientIsWoman: true, AgeInYears: 50} with LivingWithHIV absent, because the over-49 rule (DecisionRule_1l3rrvn) no longer overlaps a catch-all. In-range null-HIV inputs now return empty outputs, which is the guideline's silence made visible.
  • Purposive DMN change (applied). In purposive.dmn#CC.A.eligibility_p, the over-49 rule DecisionRule_1l3rrvn was narrowed from LivingWithHIV = - to LivingWithHIV = not(null), so that null-HIV at any age is handled exclusively by the retained catch-all DecisionRule_catchall_s_nullhiv. The catch-all's output stays (EligibleForScreening = false, ScreeningInterval = null), encoding the fail-closed policy at every age band. This was selected over narrowing the catch-all's age input because it localises the fail-closed policy to a single rule this entry can point at, rather than splitting responsibility across two age-band-specific rules.
  • Behaviour surface. After the DMN changes, the Eligibility decision behaves as follows on null LivingWithHIV:
    • Textualist: null HIV with age in the general-population or WLHIV age bands returns null outputs (no rule matches). Null HIV with age >49 returns (false, null) via the over-49 rule. This is the guideline's silence made visible.
    • Purposive: null HIV at any age returns (false, null) — a deterministic fail-closed response at every age band.
  • CDD update (Row 3 — Living With HIV) (applied). An explicit Input Option Semantics line was added clarifying what false means operationally (documented-negative or presumed-negative-at-encounter), acknowledging the three real-world states this entry enumerates, and cross-referencing cc-008 for the null-handling policy. The existing cross-reference to cc-002 remains; the two entries cover different concerns (stratification vs. missingness).
  • Interaction with cc-002. cc-002 selects the patient-level boolean as the stratification encoding and flags the null-HIV catch-all as requiring audit during purposive authoring. cc-008 is that audit's resolution. The line in cc-002 Implementation Notes that flags the audit should cross-reference this entry rather than leave the item open.
  • Interaction with cc-000. cc-000 names the moderate-to-high HIV prevalence epidemiological regime as a foundation assumption. That regime is the reason fail-closed is the selected policy here: in a low-prevalence regime, null-routes-to-general-pop would be a more defensible default. Country adaptations targeting low-prevalence settings should revisit this entry.
  • L3 integration. In the declared regime, HIV status will typically arrive at the screening encounter from a separate HIV care system (SmartCare, HIV-module-in-OpenMRS, standalone HIV EMRs). The fail-closed policy is only as useful as the L3 data-linkage step that populates LivingWithHIV before eligibility is evaluated. L3 implementers should consider the fail-closed response an actionable signal to surface a "HIV status required" prompt or workflow handoff rather than a silent recommendation of no-screening.
  • Scope: Eligibility gate only. cc-008's policy applies at the Eligibility decision (CC.A.eligibility_*), which is the cascade's entry gate for LivingWithHIV. Under the DRD, the cascade contract is Eligibility → Due for Screening → Triage → Treatment → Follow-up, with downstream decisions consuming HIV status only after Eligibility has resolved. In normal cascade execution, a null-HIV client is refused at Eligibility and never reaches Triage or Follow-up, so the fail-closed policy at Eligibility is sufficient to govern the cascade's behaviour for absent HIV status. LivingWithHIV also feeds the Triage decision's VIA-negative re-screen interval rules (DecisionRule_triage_neg_gen, DecisionRule_triage_neg_hiv), but "fail-closed" does not transfer cleanly to a decision that is setting a follow-up interval after the client has already been screened and triaged; a separate interpretive question (likely specification or inference) governs what to do if Triage is invoked directly with null HIV, and should be addressed in its own register entry if it becomes a live concern. cc-008 explicitly does not cover direct invocation of downstream decisions with null HIV.
  • Future-revision trigger. If WHO issues guidance on how to handle absent HIV status in cervical cancer screening (as part of a future cervical cancer guideline revision or as a horizontal digital health guidance), this entry becomes superseded and the policy flips to whatever WHO specifies. The evidence_volatile consideration here is lower than in cc-002 because the question is operational rather than clinical, but the trigger remains a future WHO or IARC pronouncement rather than evidence from ESTAMPA or cohort studies.
